# 4.2 Migration in Bulk Solution

In the bulk solution (away from an electrode), concentration gradients are generally small, and the total current is carried essentially entirely by migration. All charged species contribute. For species $j$ in the bulk of a linear system having a cross-sectional area $A, i_{j}=i_{\mathrm{m}, j}$. Rearrangement of (4.1.17) yields

$$
\begin{equation*}
i_{j}=\frac{z_{j}^{2} F^{2} A D_{j} C_{j}}{R T} \cdot \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x} \tag{4.2.1}
\end{equation*}
$$

By substitution from (4.1.11), $i_{j}$ can be reexpressed as

$$
\begin{equation*}
i_{j}=\left|z_{j}\right| F A u_{j} C_{j} \frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x} \tag{4.2.2}
\end{equation*}
$$

For a linear electric field, such as exists between two parallel-plate electrodes,

$$
\begin{equation*}
\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial x}=\frac{\Delta E}{l} \tag{4.2.3}
\end{equation*}
$$

[^1]where $\Delta E / l$ is the gradient $(\mathrm{V} / \mathrm{cm})$ arising from the change in potential $\Delta E$ over distance $l$. Thus,
$$
\begin{equation*}
i_{j}=\frac{\left|z_{j}\right| F A u_{j} C_{j} \Delta E}{l} \tag{4.2.4}
\end{equation*}
$$
and the total current in bulk solution due to migration is given by
$$
\begin{equation*}
i=\sum_{j} i_{j}=\frac{F A \Delta E}{l} \sum_{j}\left|z_{j}\right| u_{j} C_{j} \tag{4.2.5}
\end{equation*}
$$
which is (4.1.18) expressed for this situation.
The conductance of the solution, $G\left(\Omega^{-1}\right)$, which is the reciprocal of the resistance, $R(\Omega)$, is given by Ohm's law,
$$
\begin{equation*}
G=\frac{1}{R}=\frac{i}{\Delta E}=\frac{F A}{l} \sum_{j}\left|z_{j}\right| u_{j} C_{j}=\frac{A}{l} \kappa \tag{4.2.6}
\end{equation*}
$$
where $\kappa$, the conductivity ( $\Omega^{-1} \mathrm{~cm}^{-1}$; Section 2.3.3), is
$$
\begin{equation*}
\kappa=F \sum_{j}\left|z_{j}\right| u_{j} C_{j} \tag{4.2.7}
\end{equation*}
$$

Equally, one can write an equation for the solution resistance in terms of $\rho$, the resistivity ( $\Omega \mathrm{cm}$ ), where $\rho=1 / \kappa$ :

$$
\begin{equation*}
R=\frac{\rho l}{A} \tag{4.2.8}
\end{equation*}
$$

The fraction of the total current that a given ion, $j$, carries is $t_{j}$, the transference number of $j$ [see also (2.3.11) and (2.3.18)]:

$$
\begin{equation*}
t_{j}=\frac{i_{j}}{i}=\frac{\left|z_{j}\right| u_{j} C_{j}}{\sum_{k}\left|z_{k}\right| u_{k} C_{k}}=\frac{\left|z_{j}\right| C_{j} \lambda_{j}}{\sum_{k}\left|z_{k}\right| C_{k} \lambda_{k}} \tag{4.2.9}
\end{equation*}
$$

where the $\lambda$-values are equivalent ionic conductivities (Section 2.3.3).
